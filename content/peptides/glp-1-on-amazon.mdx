## What Amazon does NOT sell legitimately

The trap on Amazon — and the reason this article exists — is that
search results for "GLP-1" or "semaglutide" on the Amazon storefront
will surface third-party listings that look like the real thing but
aren't:

- **"Semaglutide research peptide" vials** — sold as "for laboratory
  research only, not for human consumption." Not a legal prescription
  drug. Anyone using them as medication is on their own.
- **"GLP-1 weight loss patches"** — there is no FDA-approved transdermal
  GLP-1 product. These are not therapeutic.
- **"Berberine GLP-1 booster" / "natural GLP-1" supplements** —
  supplements that claim to "stimulate GLP-1" naturally. They are not
  prescription drugs and won't produce GLP-1-class weight loss.
- **"GLP-1 shots" listings under personal-care** — usually saline,
  vitamin B12 kits, or research peptide vials in disguise.

If a listing isn't going through Amazon Pharmacy (with a prescription
required at checkout) and isn't from Amazon One Medical, it's not a
legitimate pharmaceutical product.

Yes. Amazon One Medical is a licensed primary-care provider, and Amazon

US-licensed prescribers and pharmacies.

**Can I buy GLP-1 on Amazon without a prescription?**
No. Anything that *looks* like a GLP-1 on Amazon without a prescription
requirement is not a pharmaceutical product — usually a "research
peptide" or a supplement.

**How does Amazon's $25/month figure work?**
That's the rate when manufacturer copay programs apply on top of
commercial insurance coverage. It's not a flat discount Amazon controls
— Amazon Pharmacy auto-applies the copay coupon at checkout.

**Will my insurance cover Amazon Pharmacy?**
Most major insurance plans, yes. Coverage depends on whether your
specific plan covers the prescribed GLP-1.

**Can I cancel Amazon's GLP-1 program?**
There's no membership lock-in. Visits are billed as they occur.

**Is the "Wegovy from Amazon" the same as "Wegovy from CVS"?**
Yes. Amazon Pharmacy is licensed to dispense the manufacturer-supplied
brand-name medication. Same drug, same lot system, same
manufacturer-approved cold chain.

Semaglutide is the GLP-1 receptor agonist that took the class
mainstream. Three brand names use the same active molecule for three
purposes: **Ozempic** for type 2 diabetes by injection, **Wegovy** for
chronic weight management by injection, and **Rybelsus** for type 2
diabetes by oral pill. A fourth — **oral Wegovy** — added weight-
management dosing in pill form in December 2025.

Same molecule, different doses, different indications, different
prices, and different insurance coverage. This guide covers all four.

## The molecule

| Aspect | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Generic name | Semaglutide |
| Maker | Novo Nordisk |
| Class | GLP-1 receptor agonist (single receptor) |
| Half-life | ~7 days |
| First FDA approval | 2017 (Ozempic) |

Semaglutide is a peptide-based GLP-1 agonist engineered for a long
half-life. It binds the GLP-1 receptor in the pancreas, brain, GI
tract and elsewhere — producing the standard GLP-1 effects of
appetite suppression, slowed gastric emptying, glucose-dependent
insulin release and glucagon suppression.

For mechanism details, see
[GLP-1 receptor agonists](/peptides/glp-1-receptor-agonists).

## The four brands

### Ozempic — for type 2 diabetes

- **Form:** Weekly subcutaneous injection
- **Doses:** 0.25 / 0.5 / 1.0 / 2.0 mg
- **FDA-approved:** 2017
- **Trial program:** SUSTAIN
- **A1c reduction:** ~1.5-2.0 percentage points at top dose
- **Weight loss (T2D trials):** 8-14% body weight (off-label for weight
  management without T2D is common)
- **Cardiovascular benefit:** SUSTAIN-6 demonstrated MACE reduction in
  T2D patients with high CV risk
- **List price:** ~$968-$1,028/month

### Wegovy — for chronic weight management

- **Form:** Weekly subcutaneous injection
- **Doses:** 0.25 / 0.5 / 1.0 / 1.7 / 2.4 mg (plus Wegovy HD 7.2 mg)
- **FDA-approved:** 2021 (chronic weight management); 2024 (CV risk
  reduction in obesity with CVD); 2024–25 (MASH with moderate-to-
  advanced fibrosis)
- **Trial program:** STEP (weight), SELECT (CV outcomes)
- **Average weight loss:** ~15% body weight at 68 weeks (STEP-1)
- **List price:** ~$1,349/month; NovoCare direct ~$349/month;
  commercial insurance + savings card as low as $25/month

### Rybelsus — for T2D (oral)

- **Form:** Once-daily oral tablet
- **Doses:** 3, 7, 14 mg
- **FDA-approved:** 2019
- **How to take:** Empty stomach, first thing in the morning, with ≤4 oz
  water, at least 30 minutes before food/other meds
- **A1c reduction:** ~1.0-1.5 percentage points at top dose
- **Weight loss:** 6-9 lb on average — less than the injection forms
- **List price:** ~$999/month

### Wegovy oral — for weight management (oral)

- **Form:** Once-daily oral tablet
- **Doses:** 0.25 / 0.5 / 1.5 / 4 / 9 / 14 / 25 mg
- **FDA-approved:** December 2025
- **How to take:** Same as Rybelsus — empty stomach, before food
- **Average weight loss:** ~13% body weight at top dose
- **List price:** Introductory $149-$199/month via NovoCare; $349
  standard

The empty-stomach administration is the biggest practical drawback of
oral semaglutide. The drug is highly susceptible to digestion by
stomach acid and enzymes, so food triggers degradation before the
medication is absorbed.

## How semaglutide works

The same as other GLP-1 receptor agonists:

1. **Glucose-dependent insulin release** — stimulates pancreatic beta
   cells when blood sugar rises
2. **Glucagon suppression** — reduces liver glucose output
3. **Slowed gastric emptying** — extends satiety, flattens post-meal
   glucose spikes
4. **Central appetite suppression** — reduces hunger and "food noise"

Net result: lower blood sugar, reduced appetite, weight loss.

## When semaglutide starts working

- **Within 1-2 weeks:** Reduced appetite and food noise — often
  noticeable within days
- **Weeks 1-4 (starter dose):** Modest weight loss (~2-4 lb)
- **Weeks 5-20 (dose escalation):** Steady weight loss, side effects
  flare with each dose increase
- **5-6 months:** Most weight loss occurs in this window for typical
  responders
- **6-12 months:** Continued slower loss; approaching plateau

Most patients reach the 2.4 mg target dose by week 17, after the
graded titration schedule.

## Side effects

Standard GLP-1 profile:

- **Common (40-70%):** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, reduced
  appetite, fatigue
- **Less common:** Reflux, injection-site reactions, hair thinning,
  gallbladder issues
- **Rare:** Pancreatitis, severe allergic reactions
- **Boxed warning:** Thyroid C-cell tumors (rodent data); contraindicated
  in personal/family history of MTC or MEN-2

## Semaglutide vs tirzepatide

Most asked comparison:

| Aspect | Semaglutide | Tirzepatide |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Class | Single GLP-1 | Dual GLP-1/GIP |
| Maker | Novo Nordisk | Eli Lilly |
| Average weight loss (top dose) | ~15% | ~21% |
| Half-life | ~7 days | ~5 days |
| Approved CV benefit | Yes (Wegovy) | Suggestive, data maturing |
| Approved MASH benefit | Yes (Wegovy) | Under investigation |
| Approved OSA benefit | No | Yes (Zepbound) |

Tirzepatide produces greater weight loss on average. Semaglutide has
the strongest CV evidence so far.

## FAQ

**Is semaglutide a GLP-1?**
Yes — semaglutide is the most prescribed GLP-1 receptor agonist
worldwide.

**Is Ozempic the same as semaglutide?**
Same active ingredient. Ozempic is one brand of semaglutide;
Wegovy and Rybelsus are others.

**Which semaglutide is FDA-approved for weight loss?**
Wegovy (injection) and Wegovy oral (pill). Ozempic and Rybelsus are
approved for diabetes; off-label use for weight loss is common.

**Why can semaglutide be taken as a pill but other GLP-1s can't?**
Most other GLP-1 peptides degrade too quickly in the stomach.
Semaglutide's pill version uses a special excipient (SNAC) that
protects the peptide briefly enough for absorption — but only when
taken on an empty stomach.

**How much weight can you lose on semaglutide?**
Average weight loss is ~15% body weight on Wegovy 2.4 mg at 68 weeks.
Individual results vary substantially.
